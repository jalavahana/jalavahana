Abstract

Climate change and increasing energy consumption are global concerns. In 2018, energy consumption increased by 2.9%, while carbon emissions increased by 2.0% (0.6 gigatons) (Noviani, 2019). Wind energy, through Wind Power Plants (PLTB), can help reduce carbon emissions. PLTB consists of two types: onshore and offshore, with offshore wind being stronger and more stable although the construction costs are higher. According to Government Regulation No. 79 of 2014, the target for the renewable energy mix in 2025 is 23% and 31% in 2050, with a target PLTB capacity of 255 MW in 2025. Until 2020, the installed PLTB had only reached 135 MW (KESDM, 2021). Indonesia is included in 56 potential countries for offshore wind turbines with a total potential of 227 GW. Annual wind speeds in Indonesia range from 3-6 m/s, with a minimum of 5 m/s required for turbines (Hidayat, 2022). Seabed conditions and identification of marine biota are essential for the safety and sustainability of the ecosystem. Selecting the right location is crucial for the construction of a PLTB wind turbine. The innovation needed is a measuring instrument that can review sea depth, wind potential, seabed conditions, and biological identification. Therefore, through the Karsa Cipta Student Creativity Program (PKM-KC), "Jalavahana: Unmanned Autonomous Surface Vehicle for Offshore Wind Turbine Development Planning Data Survey" will be created.

Webbased Graphical User Interface

The Webbased GUI is designed for a web-based control system that facilitates command input for the Jalavahana vessel. This GUI includes several functions, namely:

  • Displaying information in the form of tables of geographical data, wind speed, and wind direction from the vessel's sensor data.
  • Monitoring the underwater camera object detection of the Jalavahana vessel.
  • Providing navigation signals based on waypoint coordinates using the OpenStreetMap API.
  • Displaying the activity status of the Jalavahana vessel.
  • Issuing navigation commands for the Jalavahana vessel through control buttons.
  • Providing a simple visualization of the dataset collected by the vessel's sensors when the “Visualization Data” button is pressed.
